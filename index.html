EVision: AI-Powered EV Range Prediction

Making electric vehicle adoption data-driven, not guesswork.

The Business Problem

Range anxiety, the fear of running out of charge before reaching a destination is the single largest psychological barrier to EV adoption globally. Manufacturers quote idealised ranges under perfect conditions. Real-world drivers experience something entirely different. The gap erodes trust and stalls the clean-energy transition.

Why It Matters

Africa's energy transition depends on accurate, contextualised EV data. A reliable prediction tool directly supports fleet operators, policymakers, and individual buyers in making evidence-based decisions. Better range accuracy = faster EV uptake = measurable reduction in transport emissions.

The Dataset & Approach

Multivariate real-world EV performance data covering battery specifications, ambient temperature, driving speed profiles, terrain gradient, and usage patterns. Built and evaluated three regression architectures — Linear, Ridge, and Gradient Boosting — using cross-validated train/test splits and rigorous feature engineering.

The Key Insight

Battery capacity alone explains ~84% of range variance across the dataset, finding that upends conventional wisdom placing equal weight on aerodynamics, driver behaviour, and climate. This concentration of explanatory power allowed dramatic model simplification without sacrificing precision, making the model cheaper to retrain and easier to interpret.

How the Model Works

Think of it like a smart calculator that has studied thousands of real EV journeys. You give it your battery size, temperature, and driving speed. It outputs a realistic range — not a manufacturer's best-case estimate. The Gradient Boosting model learns from patterns in the data the way an experienced driver learns from experience: iteratively, by correcting its own mistakes.

Deployment

The model was wrapped in a FastAPI REST endpoint and integrated into a mobile application, enabling real-time inference for end users. This is a full ML pipeline from data ingestion, feature engineering, model training, serialisation, API layer, and client-facing app design. It's not a notebook exercise.

Mobile Money Intelligence Platform

Decoding the financial behaviour of 100M+ unbanked Africans.

The Business Problem

Mobile money has become the primary financial infrastructure for hundreds of millions of people across Sub-Saharan Africa — yet most operators rely on aggregated reports that obscure the behavioural patterns driving growth, churn, and fraud. Decision-makers are flying blind in a market growing at 20%+ annually.

Why It Matters

Fraud in mobile money directly harms the most financially vulnerable populations. Simultaneously, understanding usage patterns enables operators to extend credit, design better products, and reach underserved communities. The economic impact of getting this right is enormous and the cost of getting it wrong is borne by those least able to afford it.

The Dataset & Approach

Large-scale anonymised transaction records analysed for temporal patterns, value distribution, frequency clustering, and network anomalies. Applied statistical outlier detection and cohort segmentation to surface behavioural fingerprints across user types — merchants, individuals, and bulk senders.

Key Insights

Transaction velocity spikes during specific 47-minute windows align with agricultural payment cycles — a non-obvious pattern that, when missed, triggers false fraud flags affecting legitimate farmers. Irregular round-number transactions above a threshold proved a statistically significant indicator of structuring behaviour consistent with money laundering.

PCA: Cost of Healthy Diet in Africa

Cutting through 30 variables to find what actually drives food insecurity.

The Business Problem

Food security policymakers face a curse of dimensionality: dozens of economic indicators, food price indices, and demographic variables — but no clear picture of which levers to pull. Multicollinearity makes traditional regression unreliable. Governments and NGOs need a cleaner analytical lens.

Why It Matters

The cost of a healthy diet is a UNESCO SDG2 indicator tracking global food security progress. Understanding what drives cost variance across African nations directly informs where to direct aid, trade policy, and infrastructure investment. This is economics with human stakes.

The Dataset & Approach

Multi-country panel data spanning 30+ economic features across African regions. I implemented PCA from mathematical first principles — not just calling sklearn.decomposition. Covariance matrix construction, eigendecomposition, explained variance ratios — every step written from scratch to ensure deep interpretability.

Key Insight

Principal components revealed that infrastructure access and currency volatility collectively explain more diet-cost variance than food production metrics — a counterintuitive finding with direct policy implications: investing in roads and monetary stability may improve food security more efficiently than agricultural subsidies alone.
